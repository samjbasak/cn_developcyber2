    • A “Cyber Threat” is when there is a possibility of a malicious attempt to disrupt and/or compromise a computer network or system. Cyber threats vary in their nature from pure nuisance and minimum inconvenience to more serious data and financial losses.

    For example, typical cyber threats are of the following nature: -

    1. VIRUSES and MALWARE : Computer viruses, data breaches and Denial of Service(DoD) threats are very common resulting in the following: -
    2. POP-UPS : These may be just pure nuisance where one can close the pop-up or set the pop-up configurations to OFF and run virus scanning.
    3. HIJACKING OF A COMPUTER : In such instances, data may be lost, compromised, corrupted and deleted. Transactions may be carried out to cause financial loss. Some of it is sold onto the dark web where credit card details are used to commit fraud.
    4. SOCIAL ENGINEERING : Peopple are tricked into giving out information. This enables hacker to hack. Sensitive data is stolen and used as a basis of ransom demands. Sometimes, the computer system is brought to a total halt and to get it back up and running, one needs to pay the ransom.
